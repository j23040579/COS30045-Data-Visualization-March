# COS30045 – Data Visualisation

# Data Story

## Audience

The primary audience for this visualisation is household consumers and people looking to purchase a new television. This includes families, students, and general consumers who are upgrading to larger screen TVs for better viewing experiences.

## Audience Interest

Consumers are often drawn to larger TVs for better entertainment, but they may not be aware of the impact on electricity consumption and long-term costs. Most buyers focus on screen size, price, and brand, while energy efficiency is often overlooked or misunderstood.

# About the Data

## Data Source

The dataset used in this project is based on publicly available television product data in Australia, likely sourced from an energy efficiency registry such as the Equipment Energy Efficiency (E3) Program. It contains information about different TV models, including screen size, technology type, energy consumption, and star ratings.

## Data Processing

- Removing missing or incomplete entries
- Standardising column names (e.g., screen size, power consumption)
- Converting data types (e.g., strings to numerical values)
- Filtering relevant variables such as screen size, energy consumption, and energy rating

## Privacy

The dataset does not contain any personal or sensitive information. It only includes product-level data (e.g., TV specifications and energy usage), meaning there are no privacy risks associated with its use.

## Accuracy and Limitations

- The data may not include all TV models available in the market
- Some values may be outdated depending on the year of registration
- Energy consumption figures are based on standard testing conditions, which may differ from real-world usage
- External factors (e.g., brightness settings, usage time) are not included

## Ethics

This project aims to promote responsible and informed consumer decisions regarding energy usage. The analysis avoids misleading interpretations and presents findings honestly.
